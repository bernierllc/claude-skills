Decisions & Feedback
  ✓ The team, including Marc, Matt Bernier, and Steve M., discussed project progress with Stephanie Massei, incorporating prototype feedback from Eric and focusing on building the application environment while prioritizing core Minimum Viable Product (MVP) features, as Clay Unicorn advised against complex features like free-form questions now. Stephanie Massei provided detailed input on General Contractor (GC) needs, including the ability to focus on compliance review, required subcontractor fields like billing contact and EMR rating, and necessary features for the GC dashboard, such as a review queue, automated compliance reports, and the ability to search and bulk download documents for projects, especially for litigation support. Marc agreed to implement many of the required features, while Matt Bernier confirmed the feasibility of a single-user Procore integration and the plan to include a comprehensive audit log and automated claims report generation.
  ✓ Required Subcontractor Contact Information Stephanie Massei emphasized the importance of capturing a billing or accounting contact for subcontractors, as that person often handles insurance matters (00:14:14). Marc agreed to add "billing contact" to the subcontractor onboarding flow within the company information section as a requirement (00:15:13).
  ✓ EMR Rating Requirement Stephanie Massei introduced the requirement to disclose the Experience Modification Rate (EMR) as an indicator of a subcontractor's loss history and risk level, which would be helpful since loss runs are not typically available. Marc agreed to make the disclosure of EMR rating mandatory (00:32:59).
  ✓ Multiple License Entry and CSI Codes Stephanie Massei noted the importance of ensuring a subcontractor has the correct license for the trade they are performing, especially if they are bidding on multiple scopes of work (e.g., a landscaper bidding on concrete) (00:34:49). Clay Unicorn proposed implementing a grouped field with a plus button to allow subcontractors to enter and upload multiple licenses (00:37:09). Marc agreed to add a CSI codes mapping dropdown in the trade capabilities and experience section (00:38:05).
  ✓ Dashboard Modification and Task Focus Stephanie Massei suggested that the dashboard should be modifiable, as the current view is heavily focused on tasks, which may not be as relevant for a General Contractor (GC) perspective. Marc agreed to make the dashboard "more relatable" and focus more on existing issues and how to rectify them, rather than strictly tasks (01:00:40) (01:03:41). Stephanie Massei clarified that tasks are better suited for brokers and subcontractors, while GCs need snapshots and status updates to monitor compliance (01:04:45).
  ✓ Linking Compliance to Consequences Matt Bernier stressed the importance of linking tasks to the consequences of non-compliance, such as withholding payment or preventing site access, to make the requirements "real" for subcontractors (01:05:28). Stephanie Massei agreed, noting that consequences apply to both ongoing work (not getting paid) and bidding processes (incomplete bids) (01:07:17). Matt Bernier suggested programmatic methods to make these consequences clear in the system (01:06:17).
